# Public Due Diligence Case Patterns

Use these public-report patterns to adapt the diligence report style. Do not copy client-specific wording; use the patterns as structure and judgment discipline.

## Case Lessons

| Case | Reusable pattern | Use in this skill |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Deloitte Project Drake draft findings | Scope/basis first, use restrictions, data-room limits, QoE, normalized EBITDA, working capital, tax exposures, follow-up list | Add transaction-level findings, adjusted economics, deal issue language, and open items |
| Deloitte Project Global report | Important notice, cut-off date, scope, management access, VDR reliance, management accounts analysis | State scope, information sources, cut-off date, limitations, and management-account reliability |
| CDECCA Acquisition DD Final Report | Transaction expression: finding, price/term impact, recommended action | Convert issues into price adjustment, escrow, condition precedent, covenant, indemnity, or walk-away item |
| ADB Chennai Metro EDDR | Introduction, purpose, project status, safeguard findings, institutional setup, GRM, consultations, conclusions | Add project-status, compliance, institutional, stakeholder, and recommendation sections |
| World Bank Chhattisgarh Solar + Battery ESDDR | Methodology, document review, stakeholders, baseline profile, impact assessment, legal matrix, ESMP, gap analysis, responsibility schedule | Add external-risk layer, regulatory matrix, implementation responsibilities, and action plan |
| World Bank Poloniny National Park Trail DD | Situation analysis, stakeholder map, resource/infrastructure gaps, risks, next-step recommendations | Add resource-gap, infrastructure-gap, community/stakeholder, and practical next-step analysis |

## Four-Layer Diligence Model

### 1. Transaction / Investment Judgment

Decide whether the transaction should proceed, at what valuation or cooperation scale, and under what protections.

Required outputs: recommendation, deal breakers, valuation reasonableness, price/term implications, and follow-up items that must be resolved before signing or funding.

### 2. Business-Plan Reality

Test whether the plan can produce real revenue, margin, and cash flow.

Required checks: market definition, TAM/SAM/SOM, customer evidence, pricing model, unit economics, BOM, gross margin, working capital, capacity, supply chain, channel, sales cycle, and growth assumptions.

### 3. Execution / Governance

Test whether the organization can execute and whether records support the claims.

Required checks: team completeness, key-person dependency, governance, financial records, audit status, management accounts, internal controls, data-room completeness, legal entity, cap table, contracts, licenses, IP, labor, tax, project management, and responsibility matrix.

### 4. External Risk

Test risks outside the company model that can block or reprice the deal.

Required checks: regulatory, permitting, policy, data, export-control, safety, cybersecurity, environmental, social, land, community, labor, health and safety, ESG, stakeholder, grievance, mitigation, owner, deadline, residual risk, and monitoring requirements.

## Finding Language

For material issues, prefer this structure:

```text
Finding: What we observed.
Evidence: Source and evidence quality.
Transaction impact: How it affects valuation, terms, timing, or proceed/no-proceed judgment.
Recommended action: What to request, test, negotiate, reserve, or make a condition precedent.
Status: resolved / open / deal breaker / monitor.
```

Chinese report wording:

```text
发现问题：观察到的事实。
证据：来源与证据等级。
交易影响：对估值、条款、时间线或推进决策的影响。
建议动作：需要要求、验证、谈判、预留或设为先决条件的事项。
状态：resolved / open / deal breaker / monitor。
```

## Limitation Language

Always disclose scope, cut-off date, materials reviewed and not reviewed, whether management or third parties confirmed the information, data-room gaps, external validation gaps, and that forward-looking projections are not assured unless independently verified.
